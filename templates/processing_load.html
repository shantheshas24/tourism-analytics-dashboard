Key Insight:
Pandas outperforms Spark on a single machine due to Spark's JVM startup overhead and inter-process serialisation costs. However, in a real Hadoop/YARN/Kubernetes cluster, Spark sessions are persistent (no per-job startup cost), and work is parallelised across many CPU cores or worker nodes — making Spark significantly faster for 100M+ row datasets.

Scalability Argument:
This analysis uses a 1.48M-row dataset. Spark's architecture scales linearly — add 10 worker nodes and throughput increases ~10×. Pandas is memory-bound and single-threaded per operation. At 10–100 million rows, Spark becomes the only practical choice.
